Breastfeeding

It also very important that immediately after the birth, natural or caesarean, the little one is breastfeed as it helps him/her settling down. Breastfeeding helps both, the little one and mother, in various ways. For the little one it shown to reduce the risk of:

  • Physiological reflux,
  • Pyloric stenosis,
  • Gastro-intestinal illnesses,
  • Respiratory illnesses,
  • Ear infection,
  • Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS),
  • Necrotising enterocolitis in preterm infants,
  • Eczema,
  • Asthma,
  • Some childhood cancers,
  • Type 1 and type 2 diabetes,
  • Coeliac diseases,
  • Inflammatory bowel diseases,
  • Cardiovascular heart diseases,
  • Obesity in childhood and later in life, therefore it is recommended to do everything it takes to breastfeed your baby.

Baby safety

From the moment the baby arrives in your life will require two important things food, and sleep, whilst breastfeeding will take care of his first need, the second one will require attention in getting the cot ready. Having the baby sleep in a safe cot next to your bed for the first six to twelve months will reduce the risk of SIDS. Considering that you should:

  • have the baby sleeping on their back,
  • never sleep with your baby on a sofa or armchair,
  • not letting your baby get too hot or cold,
  • keep your baby's head uncovered. Their blanket should be tucked in no higher than their shoulders,
  • place your baby in the "feet to foot" position, with their feet at the end of the cot or Moses basket.
