Getting ready for the birth

A beautiful and special human being is going to popup in your life soon and there are some important things to consider when planning the birth of your baby.

Getting ready

It's time for you to make important decisions, as where and how you would like to give birth, making a birth plan and let your midwife or doctor to know what kind of care you would like in labour, birth and afterwards, and an essential and delicate thing that you should considering carefully is breastfeeding. But before you make a plan, you need to know more about what involves and what choices you have. You can find out more by attending antenatal classes, reading about birth and talking to other mothers or your partner, family and friends about it. The most important information you would like to know more about are:

  • labour - considering C-section caesarean, in case is not planed from the beginning,
  • breastfeeding
  • safety - first-aid,
  • sleep,
  • emergency contact numbers and where you can ask for help in diferent situations,
  • early parenting.

Having the baby

The day has finally arrived! Giving bith is different for every woman, but the main signs that indicates that you are starting the labour will be regular and strong contractions, and a show (during your pregnancy, a plug of mucus sits in your cervix) which it comes away, indicating that the cervix is starting to open. Other signs might be:

  • water breaks (rupture of the membranes)
  • crampings, similar to period pain
  • backache, or an upset stomachh
  • feeling of pressure, as the baby is moving his head into the pelvis.

After giving birth
