Folic acid

Prior to pregnancy you should start taking a folic and iodine supliment. Iodine is important for the development of your's baby brain. Taking daily folic acid, before and during pregnancy, prevents the occurance of neural tube defects, such as spina bifida, in your baby and also provide the best health outcomes when the baby is growing.

Eating

Eating for two is just a myth. A woman needs around 2000 calories a day, while during pregnancy, you should increase by 200-300 calories. After giving birth, you may need up to 500 extra calories to help with milk production. Eating a well-balanced diet, including plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables you won't need any other supliments for having a healthy pregnancy.

Prenatal screening and diagnostic tests


Screening tests

First Trimester: Blood test - between 9-13 weeks (ideally 9-12 weeks) and Ultrasound.
Second Trimester: Blood test (Maternal Serum Screen) - between 14-18 weeks (ideally 15-17 weeks).

Diagnostic tests

First Trimester:

  • Blood test for Hepatitis B, HIV, Rubella, Syphilis As earley as possible in pregnancy, but can be done at any time.
  • Blood test for Thalassaemia & Sickle Cell ideally before 10 weeks.
  • Chorionic Villus Sampling - between 11-14 weeks. It is your choice whether you have the screening and/or diagnostic tests. Diagnostic testing involves either chorionic villus sampling or amniocentesis.
Second Trimester:
  • *Amniocentesis - between 15-18 weeks.
  • Routine structural ultrasound - between 18-20 weeks.
